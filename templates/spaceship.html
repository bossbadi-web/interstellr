Celestial Vista

The Celestial Vista is a 292-foot-long space pod designed for a 6-hour flight, offering low G force and an astronaut-like experience. With its panoramic glass windows and ocean landing capability, it provides an unparalleled view of Earth's beauty. Accommodating up to eight passengers, prepare for a mesmerizing journey of Earth observation from space.

Cosmic Explorer

Embark on awe-inspiring journeys to Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, and beyond aboard the Cosmic Explorer. With advanced propulsion, enhanced observation facilities, sustainable life support, adaptable mobility, and comfortable living quarters, this spaceship opens doors to the wonders of our solar system. Discover new horizons and unravel the secrets of the cosmos like never before.

Safety

Test flights are beginning soon this year to verify that we are ready to go commercial. Communications throughout the operation – between land, sea, and capsule – will be provided by Silvus Technologies, utilizing military-grade StreamCaster MANET radios and MN-MIMO waveform technology. To provide comfort and decrease the chance of health issues on the flight, the vessel will be pressurized.

Launch

We do our best to create ideal launch conditions making flights as frequent as possible. We constantly plan to move to areas of good weather and follow the sea breeze to minimize the impact of wind and minimize the likelihood of complications.

Recovery

The cone shape at the bottom of the vessel will provide flyers with a smooth landing in the water. Fast boats from Fluid Watercraft will stabilize the capsule after it lands in the water so that the Marine Spaceport can lift it from the water and take it back to a launch site.

The History of Spaceflight

Space exploration has been a part of human history for decades. In 1957, the Soviet Union launched the first artificial satellite, Sputnik 1, into orbit. This sparked a space race between the Soviet Union and the United States, leading to several significant achievements. In 1961, Yuri Gagarin became the first human to journey into space. The Zenith of the space race came in 1969, when the United States successfully landed astronauts on the moon during the Apollo 11 mission. Since then, space exploration has continued with the launch of space probes, orbiting telescopes, and space stations. Today, many countries and private companies are actively involved in space exploration, with the goal of exploring further into our solar system and beyond.

1957

Sputnik 1

The Soviet Union launches the first artificial satellite, Sputnik 1, into orbit, marking the beginning of the space age.

1961

Yuri Gagarin

Yuri Gagarin becomes the first human to journey into space, completing a single orbit of the Earth.

1969

Apollo 11

The United States successfully lands astronauts Neil Armstrong and Edwin "Buzz" Aldrin on the Moon during the Apollo 11 mission, marking the first time humans have set foot on another celestial body.

1971

Lunokhod 1

The Soviet Union's Lunokhod 1 becomes the first remote-controlled robot to explore another celestial body, landing on the Moon and transmitting data back to Earth.

1977

Voyager Missions

NASA's Voyager 1 and Voyager 2 spacecraft are launched, embarking on a mission to explore the outer planets of our solar system and beyond.

1990

Hubble Space Telescope

NASA's Hubble Space Telescope is launched, providing unprecedented views of the cosmos and revolutionizing our understanding of the universe.

1998

ISS

The first components of the International Space Station (ISS) are launched, beginning the largest collaborative space project in history.

2012

Curiosity Rover

NASA's Curiosity rover lands on Mars, equipped with more advanced scientific instruments than previous rovers, boosting our knowledge of Mars.

2014

Rosetta Spacecraft

The European Space Agency's Rosetta spacecraft becomes the first spacecraft to orbit a comet, landing its Philae probe on the surface.

2025

Artemis III Mission

Artemis III is launched by NASA, marking the first time since Apollo 11 that humans have set foot on the moon.

2029

Luna Base

The first human settlement on the moon is built for permanent occupation.

2031

Ares Mission

NASA and the ESA launch the Ares Mission in a global collaborative effort, and humans set foot on Mars for the first time ever.

2032

Present Day

Each of these milestones has pushed the boundaries of what we know about the universe and our place in it, from the first steps on the moon to the ongoing search for life beyond Earth. Space exploration has led to countless technological advancements and scientific discoveries. Today, the sky is no longer the limit.
